### Q7: What are confidence intervals and why do they matter?
```
A: Confidence intervals tell you how reliable your score is.

WITHOUT CONFIDENCE INTERVALS:
"Your score: 67"
‚Üí Is this reliable? Could it be 60? 75?
‚Üí If you re-run the test, will you get 67 again?
‚Üí Is improvement from 67 to 69 real or just noise?

You can't tell.

WITH CONFIDENCE INTERVALS:
"Your score: 67 (95% CI: 62-72, n=160)"
‚Üí We are 95% confident the true score is between 62-72
‚Üí Re-running the test will yield 64-70 (high reproducibility)
‚Üí Improvement to 74+ is real, 69 is likely noise

WHY THIS MATTERS:
‚Ä¢ Distinguish real improvements from random variance
‚Ä¢ Prove ROI with statistical significance (p<0.05)
‚Ä¢ Defend metrics to skeptical CFOs and boards
‚Ä¢ Make data-driven decisions with confidence

All AIDI scores include 95% confidence intervals.
Monitoring tools often report single-run point estimates.
```
